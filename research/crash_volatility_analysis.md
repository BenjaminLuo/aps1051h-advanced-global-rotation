# Research Note: Why Janus Doesn't "Flatline" During Crashes

It is a common misconception that a "Defensive Rotation" implies a flat, horizontal equity curve. The Janus System is designed to capture **"Crisis Alpha"**, which means it actively trades volatile safe-haven assets rather than sitting in pure cash.

## 1. Asset Component Volatility
The `BOND_UNIVERSE` in `config.py` is not composed of zero-volatility cash. It includes instruments that are highly liquid but price-sensitive:

| Asset | Type | Historical Annualized Volatility |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **SPY** (Baseline) | US Equities | ~15% |
| **TLT** | 20+ Year Treasuries | **~14–20%** (Similar to stocks) |
| **GLD** | Gold | **~15%** |
| **LQD** | Investment Grade Debt | ~8% |
| **BIL** | 1-3 Month T-Bills | **~0.1% (The actual flatline)** |

**The Momentum Effect**: During a crash, flight-to-safety often causes `TLT` and `GLD` to spike. Because Janus uses a **Momentum Selector** (63/126-day), it will naturally select these high-performing assets. While they provide protection against equity crashes, they introduce their own "Safe-Haven Volatility."

---

## 2. The 4-Week Tranche Lag
The system uses a **4-tranche overlapping ladder**. This creates a deliberate "Smoothing Effect" but introduces a physical delay in exiting the market:

- **Week 1 of Crash**: Only Tranche A rotates. **75% of the portfolio is still in Equities.**
- **Week 2**: Tranche B rotates. **50% of the portfolio is still in Equities.**
- **Week 3**: Tranche C rotates. **25% of the portfolio is still in Equities.**
- **Week 4**: Final rotation. Only now is the portfolio 100% defensive.

During the first 21 days of a crash signal, the system is purposely "bleeding out" of market risk rather than cutting it all at once to avoid being whipsawed by false signals.

> [!TIP]
> **Prototyping a "Flatline" Mode**: If you require a true zero-volatility state during crashes, we would need to restrict the `BOND_UNIVERSE` to `BIL` and `SHY` only. However, this would likely sacrifice significant "Crisis Alpha" generated by `TLT` and `GLD` during major rallies in safety.
